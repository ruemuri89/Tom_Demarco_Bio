The Origins of a Software Engineering Pioneer

Thomas A. DeMarco, born in 1940, entered software at a time when the idea of “software engineering” barely existed. A graduate of Cornell, he quickly became a leading figure at Bell Labs, helping define the discipline from the ground up.

Structured Analysis & The Rise of DFDs

In the 1970s, DeMarco helped formalize how large systems should be decomposed, modeled, and reasoned about — long before UML or modern modeling tools existed.

His book Structured Analysis and System Specification became foundational, bringing Data Flow Diagrams (DFDs) into mainstream practice.
